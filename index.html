About

What is the hackathon about?

The Umuhinzi Digital Hackathon 2026 is a one-day innovation sprint taking place at Carnegie Mellon University Africa in Kigali. It brings together students, developers, designers, researchers, and entrepreneurs to build practical digital tools for Rwanda's agriculture sector.

Agriculture is the backbone of Rwanda's economy — employing over 60% of the workforce and contributing significantly to household income and food security. Yet farmers continue to face persistent barriers: limited access to markets, restricted credit, inadequate early warning for crop threats, and digital tools that don't work in local languages or for low-literacy users.

This hackathon is designed to tackle those gaps head-on. Teams will work across five challenge themes — from UPI-linked marketplaces and agri-finance to pest early warning, harvest logistics, and inclusive farmer interfaces. The goal isn't a polished product: it's a working prototype and a compelling case for what's possible.
